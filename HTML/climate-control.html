Climate Control

How do Trees help control the climate?

Trees absorb carbon dioxide (CO2) from the atmosphere during photosynthesis and store it in their wood, leaves, and roots. This helps to reduce the concentration of CO2 in the atmosphere, which is a key contributor to global warming and climate change.

Trees provide shade and cool the air, reducing the amount of heat absorbed by buildings and paved surfaces, which can help to reduce the urban heat island effect.

Trees clean the air by absorbing pollutants such as ozone, nitrogen oxides, and sulfur dioxide through their leaves, reducing their impact on the atmosphere.

Trees play a crucial role in regulating water cycles by reducing runoff and evaporation, thereby helping to maintain a healthy water balance in the ecosystem.

Trees store carbon in their biomass, which helps to keep it out of the atmosphere where it contributes to climate change. When trees are cut down and burned, the carbon stored in them is released back into the atmosphere.
